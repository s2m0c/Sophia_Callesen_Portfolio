Auscultation Hallucination

Auscultation Hallucination focused on bringing together auditory and visual sensory inputs in a virtual reality setting. This was inspired by Jonathan Sterne's "The Audible Past," which touched on the idea that the Western world has become so fixated on the visual world, that people are leaving behind or neglecting the audible world that they submersed in. Sterne emphasizes that people should focus more on the aural senses because they offer a greater perspective that is more spherical in nature, thus allowing subjects to be better immersed within the world they reside in. To put this to the test, our group wanted to create an interactive experience that challenged the accuracy of our players' senses. We planned to create our own virtual world that consisted of 3 people standing around a 360 camera pantomiming the same action. Simultaneaously, a recording would play on loop of one of the 3 people making the sound that went along with the action being portrayed. The live player would put on a set of VR goggles and headphones that transported them into the world that was created.

Players were able to choose in between one of the categories of noises that were offered (Noises, Bodily Noises, Annoying Noises, and Motor Functions). At that point, they would guess who out of the 3 people was actually making the sound. The inspiration for the sound categories chosen came from Westerkamp in his article, "Soundwalking." Westerkamp suggested that it is important for people to first consider the sounds closest to them, or rather the sounds that their bodies make.

The ultimate objective of the project was to test whether the visual world or the aural world better enhances our understanding of the environment/world we are immersed within. Our group also wanted to see if we could utilize the visual world to trick the aural world's of participants. our project supported the idea that both the aural and visual world have the capacity to influence our understanding of the environment/world we are immersed within.


*This piece premiered at a showcase in the School of Music at Arizona State University*
