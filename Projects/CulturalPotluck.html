Cultural Potluck

"Cultural Potluck" utilized 360 camera technology to create a genuine experience that had sentiment to the members of our specific group that we hoped would resonate with the players at the live event. As a group made up of entirely BIPOC people, our group decided that we wanted to focus our project on using technology to create an experience that allowed participants to interact with "us," our backgrounds, and the food that comes from our cultures. Our group accomplished this by taking a 360 video of us having our own potluck, where we each brought foods from our respective cultures for everyone else to try. During our personal potluck, we described our ethnicities, what food we brought to share & how we made the dishes, our relations to our cultures, and the traditions we have from our cultures. We then took the footage and split it into 3 separate perspectives, so that during the in-person event, each of us was on a separate desktop monitor. We then set a table & plate, so that an audience member could come and "join in" on our potluck. They were welcomed to sit and listen in to our stories, reminisce over their own and what food they might have brought if they could, and sample the food we were eating in the video. We each made the food we broguht to our personal potluck for the live event, so that the participants could take some and eat with us, deepening the immersive component of the experience. Our intention was to create an inclusive, wlecoming environment by using new technology to create an immersive experience that drew audience members in to learn more about our respective cultures, as well as allow them to feel open to sharing their own, by "breaking the 4th wall" and forcing perspective, so that they became the 4th person & closing point in the circle that was out Cultural Potluck.


*This piece premiered at a showcase called "Mash Up 2.0 Collaboration" produced by Robert Farid Karimi in ASU Performing and Media Arts Center at Arizona State University*
